TATA History

Since established in 1868, TATA group’s core purpose is to improve the quality of life of the communities it serves globally, through long-term stakeholder value creation based on leadership with trust.

TATA group is a global enterprise headquartered in India, it comprises over 100 operating companies in more than 100 countries across six continents, exporting products and services to over 150 countries.

The revenue of TATA companies, together, was $108.78 billion in 2014-2015, with 68% of this coming from business outside of India. TATA companies employ over 611700 people worldwide.

TCS

TATA Consultancy Services is an IT, consulting and business organization, which is part of the TATA group, India’s largest industrial conglomerate.

Since established in 1968, TCS has become a global player with 145 offices in 46 countries operating in a unique Global Network Delivery Model™(GNDM™), recognized as the benchmark of excellence.

TATA Consultancy Services’ first Global Delivery Center was established in Budapest, Hungary in February 2001. The objective of this center is to provide near-shore services to our customers in Western and Eastern Europe and to customers with a global presence. Since its establishment, TCS Hungary is a strategic delivery center globally offering a broad portfolio of services.

The advantages of the Hungary GDC:

  • The country represents a dynamic business environment
  • High-quality educational institutions
  • Skilled workforce
  • Strategic location in Europe
  • Long-lasting relationships with universities worldwide

Corporate Social Responsibility

The guiding principle of the company’s Corporate Social Responsibility program is “Impact through Empowerment”, where empowerment is a process of strengthening the future day, so that risks are minimized, value is created and certainty is experienced.

About 66% of the company’s equity capital is held by philanthropic trusts, which focus on poverty alleviation, social upliftment, education and healthcare, thus wealth is returned to the society.
